Started by taking off the rotted out shelves and sanding it down.

![](https://images.kenchinpurcell.com/potting-bench-rehab-70830462847__6cfaba7b-2911-4307-a213-1bfbce0bc1f8.jpeg)

But the top was sagging

![](https://images.kenchinpurcell.com/potting-bench-rehab-img_2505.jpeg)

So I tried weighing the top down overnight

![](https://images.kenchinpurcell.com/potting-bench-rehab-img_2503.jpeg)

Which didn't do any good. Plan B did work, which was to screw two 2x4's to the underside.

![](https://images.kenchinpurcell.com/potting-bench-rehab-70838204410__92f706a3-5b87-4c0e-8a5f-47018db4476c.jpeg)

Next was water based primer, used about half of one quart.

![](https://images.kenchinpurcell.com/potting-bench-rehab-70839943961__826f18ef-372c-4f21-9969-6f436b410862.jpeg)

Then a top coat of exterior latex semi-gloss, "Red Potato" color.

![](https://images.kenchinpurcell.com/potting-bench-rehab-img_2512.jpeg)

Cut some wire shelving using an angle grinder, screwed the shelves down, and put some blocks under the feet. Scored and cut some Hardibacker cement board for a work top.

We'll see how long this lasts from June 2023.

![](https://images.kenchinpurcell.com/potting-bench-rehab-img_2527.jpeg)
